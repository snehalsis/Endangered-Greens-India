Our Cause

India is home to several endangered crops, each vital for ecosystems, culture, and medicine. Protecting them is essential to preserving biodiversity and the well-being of communities.

Red Sandalwood (Pterocarpus santalinus): Known for its medicinal properties and industrial uses, this tree is exclusive to Andhra Pradesh and crucial for forestry research.

Musli (Chlorophytum tuberosum): A medicinal plant valued for its nutrients and skin-healing properties.

Assam Catkin Yew (Taxus wallichiana): Found only in specific regions of Arunachal Pradesh, it holds ecological and economic significance.

Ebony (Diospyros ebenum): Famous for its durable timber, it supports home goods industries and local wildlife.

Addressing crop endangerment protects lives and biodiversity, ensuring a sustainable future for generations to come.
